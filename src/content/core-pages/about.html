the philosophy

How we work

The woman who walks into the clinic is the most informed customer the wellness industry has ever had. What she has not been given is the basic respect of being taken seriously.

The transition is the most under-served stage in women's health

Perimenopause begins years before the final period, and most doctors are trained to recognise the endpoint, not the long transition.

Clinically meaningful doses, named on the front of the pack

A trace of an expensive ingredient does not earn a place on a label. The dose that matters is the one in the research.

Patience over promises

Hormonal health takes weeks to shift, not days. The formulas are built for what they do over months.

The practitioner stays visible

The naturopath who formulated the product is the one teaching you about it. No marketing team in between.

Saying less, and meaning more

There is enough noise in this category. The brand earns trust by explaining, not by shouting.

About Davina

I am a naturopath. Fifteen years in practice. Forty-two now, and in perimenopause myself.

The first sign was the sleep. I would fall asleep fine and wake at 3am, every night, wide awake. I cut the wine and the wake-ups continued.

I had every textbook on the subject, and I still walked through it convinced something else was wrong with me. That is how perimenopause works.
